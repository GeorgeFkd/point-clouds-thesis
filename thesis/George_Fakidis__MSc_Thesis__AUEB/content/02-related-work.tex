\subsection{Object detection in point clouds}
Object detection in \ac{PC}s imposes extra constraints on the AI architectures developed for learning based
tasks. Order should not change the result of the network, Transformations neither(rotation,translation).
Point clouds, especially those captured by Lidar cameras are especially sparse and this should be taken
into account when designing a methodology.
\par Foundational work in object detection for \ac{PC}s has been done in \cite{PointNet: Deep Learning...}.
In this work the authors introduce PointNet, a neural network architecture that operates on \ac{PC}s
and not some other derivative form as we will see in other approaches.

\par The authors of PointNet did extra work on the architecture in \cite{PointNet++: ...} where they introduce an enhanced version of it, called PointNet++. In this work they did X,Y,Z.

Some information regarding the general form of the datasets. For face detection extra processes are required to get
access to the datasets. Datasets containing faces are mostly for tasks such as face recognition, pose estimation and other tasks that require more tuning than conventional object detection. They differ significantly from object detection datasets as they already have cropped and aligned the faces in the data without leaving the rest of the background intact.

Most datasets for object detection in point clouds are for scenarios involving self-driving vehicles and are captured by LiDAR devices. They have pedestrians as a category but not faces.
